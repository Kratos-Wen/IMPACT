A synchronized five-view RGB-D benchmark built around real assembly and disassembly of a commercial angle grinder, unifying temporal understanding, cross-view understanding, action forecasting, and state & reasoning under realistic industrial conditions.

The current public release provides benchmark protocol assets, evaluation code, and method wrappers. Paper link, teaser video, and media download endpoints will be finalized for the full release.

Assembly Task and Recording Setup

The benchmark centers on real assembly and disassembly of a commercial angle grinder with 12 components and 4 tool types, captured with synchronized ego-exo RGB-D views plus gaze, audio, and cognitive metadata.

Trials 112 recordings
Participants 13 subjects
Duration 39.5 hours
Views 1 ego + 4 exo RGB-D

Why IMPACT

Real industrial assistants must reason over viewpoint shift, partial observability, non-linear execution, and corrective behavior at the same time. IMPACT is designed around this full conjunction rather than around a single isolated task.

Deployment conditions that matter

  • Hands, tools, and object geometry create view-specific unobservability.
  • Assembly follows a partial-order prerequisite graph rather than a fixed linear script.
  • Anomalies and recovery are part of normal execution, not rare edge cases.
  • Industrial evaluation needs to separate perceptual failure from procedural failure.

What IMPACT makes measurable

  • A synchronized five-view RGB-D benchmark built on real power-tool assembly and disassembly.
  • A multi-granularity annotation hierarchy linking actions, steps, states, phases, and anomalies.
  • A unified benchmark suite where all task families share the same recordings and conditions.
  • Baseline comparisons that expose observability, reasoning, and forecasting gaps under deployment-oriented complexity.

Dataset Snapshot

IMPACT comprises 112 trials from 13 participants, totaling 39.5 hours across two angle grinder models, with synchronized ego-exo RGB-D capture, gaze, audio, and cognitive metadata.

Assembly object

Two angle grinder configurations

The dataset spans two commercial angle grinder models, providing a natural cross-configuration generalization axis.

Execution structure

Multi-route procedural execution

Valid trajectories are governed by a partial-order prerequisite graph instead of a single rigid sequence.

Supervision

Anomaly-recovery and compliance aware

The dataset includes explicit anomaly-recovery supervision, compliance phases, and a six-category anomaly taxonomy.

Human metadata

Cognitive load at trial level

Each trial is paired with NASA-TLX cognitive load measurement, connecting observable execution to human workload.

Supported Tasks

IMPACT is organized into four benchmark families: temporal understanding, cross-view understanding, action forecasting, and state & reasoning. All are instantiated on shared recordings.

Annotation Schema

The annotation hierarchy is designed so that each level addresses a distinct reasoning requirement: interaction dynamics, procedural structure, compliance, and physical state evolution.

Fine-grained actions

137 valid hand-specific action classes capture coordinated bimanual interaction.

Procedural structure

26 coarse steps and 51 completion events connect segmentation to graph-based reasoning.

Assembly states

17 component instances use ternary state supervision for direct ASR and indirect PSR.

Compliance and anomalies

Normal, anomaly, and recovery phases are paired with six non-exclusive anomaly types.

Benchmark

Baselines are organized to span complementary architectural inductive biases, making it possible to diagnose which design choices matter under industrial procedural complexity.

Released baselines

  • TAS: LTContext, ASQuery, DiffAct, FACT
  • CV: I3D, VideoMAE v2, MViTv2
  • ASR: MS-TCN++, VideoMAE v2+Head, Gemini 3.1 Pro
  • PSR: indirect and direct graph-based baselines
  • PPR / ATR: released reasoning and anomaly baselines
  • AF-S: AVT, ScalAnt, Qwen3VL-8B

Metrics

  • TAS, PPR, ATR: Accuracy, Edit, and F1@{10,25,50}.
  • CV-TA: Recall@{1,5}, Median Rank, and Exo→Ego coverage.
  • CV-SMR / CV-SMC: retrieval and classification metrics at verb, noun, and verb–noun levels.
  • AF-S: mean Top-5 Recall; AF-L: ED@5 and AUED.
  • ASR: Macro-F1, Transition F1, and Final-State Accuracy.
  • PSR: Step Completion F1, Detection Delay, and POS.
