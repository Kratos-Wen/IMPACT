A synchronized five-view RGB-D dataset and benchmark for industrial assembly under viewpoint shift, partial observability, multi-route execution, and anomaly-recovery.

Detailed protocols, released baselines, and evaluation scripts are maintained in the GitHub repository.

Why IMPACT

The benchmark is designed for deployment-oriented industrial understanding rather than for a single isolated perception task.

What makes the setting hard

  • Hands, tools, and object geometry create view-specific unobservability.
  • Assembly follows a partial-order prerequisite graph rather than a fixed linear script.
  • Anomalies and recovery are part of normal execution, not rare edge cases.

What the benchmark adds

  • A synchronized five-view RGB-D benchmark built on real power-tool assembly and disassembly.
  • A multi-granularity annotation hierarchy linking actions, steps, states, phases, and anomalies.
  • A unified suite where all task families share the same recordings and conditions.

Dataset Snapshot

IMPACT comprises 112 trials from 13 participants and 39.5 hours of synchronized industrial assembly recordings.

Assembly object

Two angle grinder configurations

Two commercial configurations provide a natural cross-configuration generalization axis.

Execution structure

Multi-route procedural execution

Valid trajectories are governed by a partial-order graph rather than a single rigid sequence.

Supervision

Anomaly-recovery and compliance aware

Explicit anomaly-recovery supervision is paired with compliance phases and a six-category anomaly taxonomy.

Human metadata

Cognitive load at trial level

Each trial is paired with NASA-TLX cognitive load measurement.

Compliance and anomalies

Benchmark

The benchmark repository groups released methods by task family and keeps detailed protocols in the task-specific pages.

Released coverage

  • TAS, CV, AF-S, ASR, PSR, PPR, and ATR are released.
  • AF-L is reserved and documented for later release.
  • Detailed model lists and launch commands live in the repository.

Metrics

  • Dense prediction metrics for temporal and compliance tasks.
  • Retrieval and classification metrics for cross-view tasks.
  • Task-specific forecasting, state, and procedural reasoning metrics.
